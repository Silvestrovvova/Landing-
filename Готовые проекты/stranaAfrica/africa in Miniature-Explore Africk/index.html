Cameroon: Africa in Miniature

Capital: Yaounde | Language: English, French | Currency: XAF

Brief History

Cameroon gained independence in 1960. Formerly a German colony, it was later divided between Britian and France. This dual heritage is key to its identity. THe country is often called **"Africa in Miniature"** due to its geographic and cultural diversity, spanning from beaches to mountains and deserts.

Top Attractions

1. Mount Cameroon

An active volcano and the highest peak in West Africa, popular for challenging hiking expeditions and offering stunning views of the coast.

2. Waza National Park

Located in the far north, this is one of the country's most famous national parks, offering excellent opportunities for safaris to see elephants, giraffes, and lions.

3. Kribi Beaches

Know for its beautiful white-sand beaches and the famous ** Lobe Waterfalls**, where the river flows directly into the Atlantic Ocean. A perfect spot for relaxation.
